Tips

If you're struggling with the game, the following advice might be useful to you.

Create a new company whenever possible. The free share you receive is often enough to give you a minority bonus, or even a majority bonus, if the company is sold quickly.

Think carefully about which company to create. If you are low on cash, create one of the cheaper companies (Eclipse Solar and Maniac Timber) so that you can buy more shares in them and have a greater chance of acquiring a majority or minority bonus. On the other hand, if you have more cash than other players, pick one of the more expensive companies (Superslick Oil and Whoops Uranium) and grow them quickly to put their shares out of reach of other players.

Don't buy more shares than is necessary. The biggest opportunity for making money in the game is through mergers, so once you hold enough shares in a company to be either the majority or minority shareholder, there is little point in buying more. 13 shares is always enough to be the majority shareholder, but in games with more than two players or games with the DoE active, a significantly lower number is usually enough.

Don't put all your faith in one company. If other players notice that you have bought up nearly all the shares in any company, it will be in their interests to make sure that company does not grow and is not taken over by any other company. They may hold tiles which would be very lucrative for you, but will have no incentive to play them.

Change your strategy as the game progresses. Initially, you will want to buy shares in companies that look like they will be taken over soon. Later in the game, if it becomes clear that one company is going to dominate the board, you may wish to buy shares in that company instead, as they will quickly grow in value.
