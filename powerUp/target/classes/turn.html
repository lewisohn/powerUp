Playing a turn

At the beginning of each turn, you will receive a random number of actions for that turn. There is a 3/6 chance you will get three actions, a 2/6 chance you will get four actions, and a 1/6 chance you will get two actions. Actions do not carry over to the next turn.

You may use your actions for many purposes, each of which is detailed below. Your first action must be either to draw tiles or play a tile.

Drawing tiles

Whenever you have less than five tiles in your hand, you may press the "Draw tiles" button to receive up to five new ones. You may never hold more than five tiles in your hand at any time.

Playing a tile

You may play a tile with your first action, or, if you chose to draw new tiles with your first action, you must play a tile with your second action.

When you hover over a tile, its location on the board will be highlighted. Clicking the tile plays it to that location.

If the tile you played has no neighbouring tiles on the board (in the four compass directions - diagonal neighbours are not counted), then it becomes a neutral tile, and nothing happens. You may use your remaining actions, if you have any, to play more tiles, to buy shares or to draw new tiles.

On the other hand, if the tile you played has at least one neighbouring tile, then one of three things - an attempt to create a new company, an expansion of an existing company, and a merger of two or more companies - will take place. These three events are detailed below. After one of them has occurred, you will no longer be able to play tiles on your current turn (unless the tile you played caused a failed attempt to create a new company), but you may use your remaining actions, if you have any, to buy shares or to draw new tiles.

Creating a new company

After playing a tile, if it has at least one neighbouring neutral tile and no neighbour tiles belonging to a company, then an attempt to create a new company will be made. This attempt will succeed if there are fewer than six active companies on the board at the time, and fail otherwise.

You may select any company to create from the list of currently inactive companies, and you will receive one free share in that company. If the DoE is in the game (see the "Overview" tab for more information on the DoE), it will receive a random number of shares in the company, at least one and no more than 10 minus the number of players in the game (e.g. in a two-player game, no more than eight).

Each company has a pre-determined starting share price. These prices are as follows:

Eclipse Solar$200
Maniac Timber$200
Cortex Power$300
Rufus Geothermal$300
Superslick Oil$400
Whoops Uranium$400

After a company has been created, it will build its headquarters at the tile you played, and expand into any neighbouring neutral tiles.

Expanding a company

If the tile you played has at least one neighbour tile belonging to a company, but does not have neighbouring tiles belonging to two different companies, then the neighbouring company will expand into the tile you played, as well as any neighbouring neutral tiles.

When a company expands, its share prices increases by $50 for each new tile it acquires. For example, the starting share price for Eclipse Solar is $200, and once it has expanded into two additional tiles, giving it a total size of four tiles, its share price will be $200 + (2 * $50) = $300.

Merging two or more companies

If the tile you played has neighbouring tiles belonging to two or more different companies, a merger will be initiated. Mergers are one of the most important elements of the game, and you can read more about them on the "Mergers" tab.

Buying shares

Once you have played at least one tile to the board, you may use your remaining actions to buy shares in the currently active companies by clicking the "Stock market" button and bringing up the shares dialog.

Each company starts with 25 shares when it is created, and no additional shares are issued when they have sold out. You cannot buy shares in inactive companies.

You may only buy a share from a company if you have enough cash to do so, and if you have actions remaining. Each purchase counts as one action, so you need three actions to buy three shares, for example.

There are two reasons to buy shares. Firstly, if you buy shares in a small company which then expands, your shares will be worth more than when you bought them. Secondly, and more importantly, if you buy enough shares to become either the largest or second largest owner of shares in any given company, you will receive a sizeable bonus if and when the company is taken over by another. You can read more about those bonuses on the "Mergers" tab.

Ending your turn

Once you have run out actions, you may not draw new tiles, play any more tiles or buy any more shares, but you can still view the stock market. Once you are ready to continue with the game, press the "End turn" button to pass the turn to the next player.
