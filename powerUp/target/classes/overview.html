Overview

powerUp is a game in which you and up to three other players compete to amass the most cash as you control the future of six energy companies.

Getting started

Choose how many players you would like in your game, from two to four. You may give each player a name or stick with the defaults.

You may also choose whether or not to play with the Department of Energy (DoE for short). The DoE is a passive player that buys shares when a new company is created and earns bonuses when they are merged, but does not compete for victory. Playing with the DoE is recommended for two-player games, but can be selected for any size of game.

Note that the use of the word "player" in these help pages does not refer to the DoE unless otherwise stated.

Initial draw

At the very beginning of the game, each player is randomly allocated one tile. These tiles determine the order of play: whichever player's tile is closest to the left side of the board goes first, and the remaining players follow in order from left to right. In case of a tie, the player whose tile is closer to the top of the board goes first.

Each player then takes their turns in order, starting with a cash balance of $6000. More information on playing a turn is available in the next tab, "Playing a turn".
